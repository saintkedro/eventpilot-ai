# Event Lifecycle

Version: 2.0

Last Updated: July 2026

---

## Purpose

Every feature in EventPilot exists to improve one or more stages of the event lifecycle.

Rather than thinking in terms of isolated features (RSVP, Ticketing, QR Check-in), EventPilot is designed around the complete journey of an event—from the first idea to planning the next one.

This lifecycle serves as the product's organizing framework.

In the WhatsApp-first model, most stages are driven by conversation. Organizers describe what they need; EventPilot executes and follows through — without requiring a dashboard.

---

# Stage 1 — Inspiration

The organizer has an idea.

Examples:

- Birthday
- Wedding
- Church Program
- Conference
- Product Launch
- Training
- Family Reunion
- Graduation

### EventPilot Responsibilities

- Ask questions
- Recommend event types
- Suggest dates
- Suggest venues
- Estimate budgets
- Recommend vendors
- Generate a planning checklist

Success Metric:

The organizer moves from "I have an idea" to "I have a plan."

---



# Stage 2 — Planning

The organizer defines:

- Date
- Time
- Venue
- Capacity
- Budget
- Speakers
- Vendors
- Guests
- Agenda

AI Responsibilities

- Generate timelines
- Identify missing tasks
- Estimate costs
- Detect scheduling conflicts
- Recommend improvements

Success Metric:

The event has a complete execution plan.

---



# Stage 3 — Creation

EventPilot creates:

- Event website
- Registration forms
- RSVP options
- Tickets
- Guest categories
- QR codes
- Calendar entries

Everything should be generated automatically wherever possible.

---



# Stage 4 — Promotion

Channels:

- WhatsApp
- Email
- SMS
- Social Media
- EventPilot Marketplace

AI can generate:

- Captions
- Flyers
- Email campaigns
- Reminder messages
- Countdown posts

Success Metric:

People become aware of the event.

# Stage 5 — Registration

- RSVP
- Buy tickets
- Ask questions
- Add companions

Organizer:

Views registrations in real time.

---



# Guiding Principle

Every new feature must improve at least one stage of the Event Lifecycle.

If it doesn't, reconsider whether it belongs in EventPilot.
